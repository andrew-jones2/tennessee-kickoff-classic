How It Works

Handicap Format

The Tennessee Kickoff Classic uses a net stroke play format based on USGA World Handicap System (WHS) indexes to level the playing field across all skill levels.

📋

Course Handicap

Each player's course handicap is calculated using the USGA formula: Handicap Index × (Slope Rating ÷ 113) + (Course Rating − Par). This is applied before each round.

⛳

Net Score

A player's net score is their gross score minus their full course handicap. Lowest net score wins. All strokes are taken on the hardest-rated holes per the scorecard.

🏆

Season Points

Points are awarded each round based on net finishing position. 1st place earns 10 pts, 2nd earns 8 pts, 3rd earns 6 pts, and so on. The season champion holds the most cumulative points.

🔄

Handicap Updates

Handicap indexes are updated after each round using posted scores. The most current USGA index on file as of the day of the event will be used — no exceptions.

Official

Rules

All play is governed by the USGA Rules of Golf with the local rules listed below. In the event of a dispute, the Tournament Committee's ruling is final.

  • USGA Rules Apply All play is conducted under the current USGA Rules of Golf unless modified by a local rule stated on the official scorecard.
  • Pace of Play Groups are expected to maintain pace with the group ahead. A group falling more than one hole behind will receive a warning; repeated delays may result in a one-stroke penalty.
  • Ready Golf Ready golf is encouraged throughout the round. Out-of-turn play is permitted in the interest of pace and will not result in a penalty.
  • Scorecards All scorecards must be signed by the player and an attesting playing partner before submission. Cards submitted with errors or unsigned will be disqualified.
  • Handicap Integrity Players are responsible for maintaining an up-to-date USGA handicap index. Sandbaggers or players with manipulated indexes will be subject to disqualification at the Tournament Committee's discretion.
  • Equipment All equipment must conform to USGA standards. Maximum 14 clubs in the bag. Violation results in a two-stroke penalty per hole, maximum four strokes.
  • Sportsmanship All participants are expected to conduct themselves with courtesy and respect. The Tournament Committee reserves the right to disqualify any player for unsportsmanlike conduct.
  • Inclement Weather In the event of lightning, play is immediately suspended. Rounds canceled due to weather may be rescheduled or declared official after 9 holes at the Committee's discretion.
