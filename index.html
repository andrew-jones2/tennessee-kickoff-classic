How It Works

Handicap & Format

The Tennessee Kickoff Classic uses the USGA GHIN app to calculate and track handicaps for the PTS Game. This year, we will be introducing the solo PTS game for all rounds. A teams game will also be played in rounds 1 & 2.

📋

PTS Calculator

36 - Player's HCP = Target # of points

⛳

Scoring

A player's score will be calcuated by total # of points. For example, if Golfer X has a 10 HCP, he needs 26 points to be even par, while 28 points would be a score of -2.

🏆

Final Scoring

You will have a score for each round. Final score will be a combination of all rounds.

🔄

Handicap Updates

Handicap adjustments will take place after each round using posted scores.

Official

Rules

All play is governed by the USGA Rules of Golf with the local rules listed below. In the event of a dispute, the Tournament Committee's ruling is final.

  • First Tee Two off the first tee – always. If taking 2, hit both balls back to back.
  • Golfer Categories The roster of players will be sorted into categories based on skill, A-D. There will be no A players paired on the same team.
  • Drawings Drawings Wednesday & Thursday night will be final. There will be no changing of Tee Times, unless otherwise approved by the Handicap Committee.
  • Lift, Clean, Place To rule out ambiguity, we will play lift clean and place. This allows players to mark their ball in fairways, rough, fescue, waste area, pines, dirt, or weeds and place it within a club length of its original resting spot. Bunkers are excluded from this list – play is as it lies. You may not move your ball any closer to the hole in this process OR move to an improved environment, i.e. moving from rough to fairway. Failure to comply with these rules will result in the assessment of a 1 stroke penalty.
  • Florida Rule To save both time and strokes, this rule refers to hitting the ball Out of Bounds or in Water. If a ball is hit OB, the golfer will drop on the nearest point of exit and take 1 stroke, rather than re-teeing. All members of the group will need to approve of the spot of the drop.
  • Turbo-Leather This rule refers to “gimme putts”. To determine if the putt is good, a player must place his clubhead in the cup and lay the putter flat on the ground toward their ball. If any part of the ball is within the leather grip of the putter, that putt is counted as good. Opposing players and teammates cannot give putts without measuring.
  • Scorecard Failure to turn in a complete & accurate score card will result in disqualification.
  • Playoff Playoff format will be sudden death under the same format listed above.
